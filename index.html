Failure Patterns Identified

Pattern 1

Tasks Marked "Done" Without Completion

VAs close tasks at 100% when the work is not finished. Cases: Cross Country Mechanical (Camila), RapidRoof, Aspire Homes, J Kern Construction GBP (new, Apr 1). Oriana admitted in the March 18 meeting this happens due to KPI pressure.

Pattern 2

No Visual QA Before Marking Done

Websites go live with wrong logos, placeholder content, old business names, broken layouts, unreadable text (Precision Carpentry, new). Cases: RapidRoof, placeholder content (Charlie's report), Rawlings Heating (Alejandra), Precision Carpentry. Kai Stone explicitly made this the VA's responsibility.

Pattern 3

Websites Built Ignoring Client Specs

VA builds the site with wrong service emphasis, wrong content focus, or generic layout rather than what the client discussed in onboarding. Cases: Castle Construction, DFW Precise Remodel, LYNQ Roofing.

Pattern 4

Assets Not Uploaded / Work Never Started

Client sends photos, VA never uploads them. Tasks assigned but no action taken for weeks. Cases: KDP Plumbing (10 photos), Roofs by Recon (2+ week delay), Andres's blocked launches.

Pattern 5

VA Handoffs Die Silently

One VA escalates to another, the receiving VA never picks it up, nobody follows up. Case: Cross Country Mechanical (Camila to Oriana).

Pattern 6

Blockers Not Communicated

VAs encounter technical issues (CSP photo bug, template domain dependency) but don't flag them. They either skip the work or launch with it broken. Only raised when forced in a group meeting.

Pattern 7

Same Defect Repeated Across Consecutive Builds New

Identical mistakes ship on multiple consecutive sites with no correction. 3 straight websites with broken Privacy Policy/Terms links (Home Suite Inc + 2 others, Apr 2). No feedback loop exists between builds. VAs are not learning from previous errors or reviewing peer work. This pattern is the strongest evidence that verbal direction alone (March 18 meeting) is insufficient - structural process changes are required.

R

Recommended Fixes

Prioritized by impact

P1

Mandatory Visual QA Checklist Before Task Closure

VAs must complete a checklist before any website task can be moved to "Done": logo correct, all photos uploaded, address correct, mobile view checked, all links functional (including Privacy Policy and Terms), all task items addressed. Attach desktop + mobile screenshots to the task.

Owner: Astrid Altuve / Kai Stone | Timeline: This week
P1

Fix CSP Task Photo Attachment Bug

Photos attached to tasks are invisible to VAs. This is causing work to be skipped or done wrong. Interim fix: all assets go in client Google Drive folder. Long-term: fix the platform bug.

Owner: Michael Vanhoutte / Umar | Timeline: This week (workaround), 2 weeks (fix)
P1

Review VA KPI Structure

If VAs are incentivized to close tasks quickly over doing them correctly, the metrics are driving the wrong behavior. Add a "reopened task" or "revision requested" metric that penalizes premature closure.

Owner: Astrid Altuve / Michael Vanhoutte | Timeline: 2 weeks
P1

GBP Optimization Definition of Done New

A GBP profile cannot be marked complete until: minimum 5 photos uploaded, business hours set, services listed, at least 1 Google post published, business description written, and all categories configured. The J Kern case proves VAs are closing GBP tasks with bare-minimum profiles.

Owner: Astrid Altuve / Ryan Grant | Timeline: 1 week
P2

Add "Blocked" Task Status in CSP

VAs must move tasks to "Blocked" (with reason) instead of leaving them in limbo or closing them incomplete. Blocked tasks trigger a notification to the CSM. Prevents silent handoff deaths.

Owner: Michael Vanhoutte (platform) | Timeline: 2 weeks
P2

Pre-Launch Checklist for All Websites

Before any website can be marked live: domain connected, review link works, GBP linked, forms functional, Privacy Policy and Terms links verified, all pages load, mobile responsive, text readable on all backgrounds. VA cannot close website task until checklist is complete.

Owner: Astrid Altuve | Timeline: 1 week
P2

VA Handoff Accountability

When a VA reassigns or escalates a task, the original VA remains accountable until the receiving VA confirms pickup. No more Camila-to-Oriana situations where work dies in the gap.

Owner: Astrid Altuve / Michael (platform) | Timeline: 2 weeks
P3

Weekly Spot-Check Audit

CSM Team Lead reviews 5 randomly selected "completed" website tasks per week. Report findings to Astrid. Creates an accountability feedback loop.

Owner: Ryan Grant + Astrid Altuve | Timeline: Ongoing weekly
